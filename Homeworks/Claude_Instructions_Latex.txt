3. CHAPTER AND SECTION HIERARCHY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

• Each homework is ONE \chapter{}
  \chapter{Homework 1}   ← in 01_chapter.tex of HWx folder

• Each homework point is ONE \section{}
  \section{Weak Form and FE Discretization}
  \section{MATLAB Solver}
  \section{Wave Reflection at an Impedance Jump}

• Sub-topics within a section use \subsection{}
  \subsection{Strong Formulation}
  \subsection{Weak Formulation}
  \subsection{Numerical Results}

• Inline highlighted paragraphs (no blank line after) use \paragraph{}
  \paragraph{Internal forcing term}
  \paragraph{Left subdomain \(x < 0.5\) — partial standing wave}

• Labels follow the pattern:
  \label{sc:HW_SECTION}    e.g. \label{sc:12}   for HW1 section 2
  \label{eq:shortname}     e.g. \label{eq:weakform}
  \label{fig:shortname}    e.g. \label{fig:fem_comp}
  \label{tab:shortname}    e.g. \label{tab:l2_errors}

4. MATHEMATICAL WRITING — CONVENTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Inline math: \( ... \) — ALWAYS, never $...$
Display math (unnumbered): \[ ... \]
Display math (numbered, referenced): \begin{equation} ... \end{equation}
In general use \underline for vectors and \mathbf for matrices

Notational shortcuts used throughout:
  u'         for spatial derivative (defined explicitly on first use)
  u'(0)      for boundary evaluation
  \mathrm{ex}  for exact solution subscript: u_{\mathrm{ex}}
  \mathbb{P}_1  for FE polynomial space
  \mathcal{M}, \mathcal{A}, \mathcal{F}  for bilinear/linear forms
  \underline{u}  for solution vector (algebraic)
  \mathbf{A}, \mathbf{M}  for matrices

Norms:
  \|u - u_h\|_{L^2(0,L)}
  \|u - u_h\|_{H^1(0,L)}
  |u|_{H^2(0,L)}

Physical units in text: use \SI{value}{unit} from siunitx package
  Example: \SI{0.4}{\meter}, \SI{5\pi}{\radian\per\meter}

Error rates: always display as \(\mathcal{O}(h^2)\) inline,
  and as the formula p = \frac{\log(e_i/e_{i+1})}{\log(h_i/h_{i+1})}

System of equations: use aligned inside \left\{ ... \right.
  \begin{equation}
  \left\{
  \begin{aligned}
    & \text{equation 1} \\
    & \text{equation 2}
  \end{aligned}
  \right.
  \end{equation}

Piecewise functions: use cases
  \begin{equation}
  f(x) =
  \begin{cases}
    \text{value 1}, & \text{condition 1} \\
    \text{value 2}, & \text{condition 2}
  \end{cases}
  \end{equation}

Implications and aligned derivations: use \quad \Longrightarrow \quad
  \(A = B \quad \Longrightarrow \quad C = D\)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. TEXT WRITING STYLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

VOICE: impersonal / passive scientific English
  CORRECT:   "The weak formulation is derived by multiplying..."
  CORRECT:   "It is convenient to identify..."
  INCORRECT: "We derive...", "I compute..."

TENSE: present simple for derivations and results
  "The boundary term reduces to..."
  "The numerical results confirm..."

INTRODUCTORY SENTENCES: each section/subsection opens with a full sentence
  that contextualizes the content without using "In this section".
  CORRECT:   "The propagation of a time-harmonic field in a heterogeneous
              medium is considered..."
  INCORRECT: "In this section, we consider..."

TRANSITIONS between sub-results: use connectors
  "Substituting into the previous expression yields:"
  "Collecting all terms, the weak formulation becomes:"
  "Therefore, ..."
  "Hence, ..."
  "It follows that ..."
  "This choice preserves the structure of..."

PHYSICAL INTERPRETATION subsections: always end with a \paragraph{} block
  per distinct region or regime. Format:
  \paragraph{Region name — physical description}
  Followed by 2–4 sentences explaining the physics.

INLINE REFERENCES: always use \ref{} and \eqref{}, never hardcode numbers
  "...as shown in figure \ref{fig:dispersion}..."
  "...from equation \eqref{eq:weak_form}..."
  "...in section \ref{sc:11}..."
  "...in chapter \ref{sc:11}..."  (if referring to the whole chapter section)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. FIGURES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Single figure:
  \begin{figure}[!htb]
      \centering
      \includegraphics[width=0.90\textwidth]{00_Images/HWx/X.Y_Name.png}
      \caption{Full descriptive caption. Include: what is shown, key
               observations, relevant parameter values.}
      \label{fig:shortname}
  \end{figure}

Two subfigures (stacked vertically):
  \begin{figure}[!htb]
      \centering
      \subfloat[Caption for subfig (a). Include observation.]
        {\includegraphics[width=0.90\textwidth]{00_Images/HWx/X.1_Name.png}}
      \\
      \subfloat[Caption for subfig (b). Include observation.]
        {\includegraphics[width=0.90\textwidth]{00_Images/HWx/X.2_Name.png}}
      \caption{Overall caption describing both panels together with
               full parameter list.}
      \label{fig:shortname}
  \end{figure}

Caption style:
  - Subfloat captions: 1–2 full sentences, describe what the panel shows
    and the key qualitative observation
  - Main caption: restate what is shown + full list of parameters used
    (domain, BCs, coefficients, N)
  - Never end a caption with a period only if it is a figure label fragment

Image width: always 0.90\textwidth for single and subfloat images.
Placement: always [!htb]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7. TABLES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Standard convergence table template:
  \begin{table}[!htb]
  \centering
  \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.4}
  \begin{tabular}{@{}cccc@{}}
  \toprule
  \(N\) & \(h\) & \(\|u - u_h\|_{L^2}\) & Rate \(p\) \\
  \midrule
  10  & \(0.1000\) & \(8.21 \times 10^{-1}\) & --- \\
  20  & \(0.0500\) & \(2.05 \times 10^{-1}\) & \(2.04\) \\
  ...
  \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
  \caption{Descriptive caption with context and parameter values.}
  \label{tab:shortname}
  \end{table}

Rules:
  - Always use booktabs (\toprule, \midrule, \bottomrule)
  - Always \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.4}
  - Use @{} to remove outer padding
  - Scientific notation: \(a.bc \times 10^{n}\)
  - First rate entry: always ---
  - Column headers in math mode if they are symbols

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
8. LISTS (itemize / enumerate)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Use \begin{itemize} for unordered conceptual lists (e.g. notational
conventions, matrix entries):

  \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Term name:}
      \[
        M_{ij} = \int_0^L \varphi_i \varphi_j dx
      \]
      Explanatory sentence after the formula.
      \item ...
  \end{itemize}

Rules:
  - Bold the term name when listing matrix/vector entries
  - Include the formula inside the item, not in a separate environment
  - Follow each formula with a sentence explaining its role
  - Max 2 nesting levels

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9. CROSS-REFERENCE DISCIPLINE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When referring backward: "...as derived in section \ref{sc:11}..."
When referring forward: "...which will be shown in section \ref{sc:14}..."
When citing an equation: "...from \eqref{eq:weak_form}..."
When citing a figure: "...in figure \ref{fig:dispersion}..."
When citing a table: "...in table \ref{tab:l2_errors}..."

NEVER write "in the previous section" without a \ref{}
NEVER write "equation (3)" hardcoded

10. SECTION STRUCTURE TEMPLATE (for each homework point)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Standard flow for a computational section:

  \section{Point Title}
  \label{sc:HX_Y}

  [1–2 sentence introduction contextualizing the problem]

  \subsection{Problem Setup / Mathematical Setting}
  [Define domain, BCs, parameters. Use equation for the strong form.]

  \subsection{Analytical / Theoretical Derivation}
  [Derive weak form, exact solution, reflection coefficient, etc.]
  [Use \paragraph{} for sub-derivations (forcing, BCs, etc.)]

  \subsection{Numerical Results}
  [Describe what was run (N, parameters). Reference the figure.]
  [The figure follows immediately after the descriptive sentence.]

  \subsection{Physical Interpretation}
  [Use \paragraph{Region/Regime — label} blocks.]
  [One paragraph per distinct behavior, 3–5 sentences each.]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11. WHAT NOT TO DO
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  ✗  Never use $...$ for inline math — use \( ... \)
  ✗  Never hardcode section/equation/figure numbers
  ✗  Never write "In this section, we..."
  ✗  Never use "we" or "I" — always passive or impersonal
  ✗  Never use \textbf{} inside equations
  ✗  Never place figures with [h] alone — always [!htb]
  ✗  Never use \\ inside text paragraphs for line breaks — use blank line
  ✗  Never leave a subsection without at least one complete sentence before
     the first formula
  ✗  Never write a caption that is only a title — always a full sentence
     with observable + parameters
